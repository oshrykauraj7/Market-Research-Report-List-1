<p><p>Vehicle fire suppression systems primarily include wet and dry systems. Wet systems utilize water or specialized foam to extinguish fires, providing effective cooling and smothering. These systems are often found in larger vehicles like buses and heavy machinery. On the other hand, dry systems deploy dry chemical agents to interrupt the combustion process, quickly suppressing flames. They are commonly used in lighter vehicles, such as passenger cars and trucks, due to their rapid response and efficiency in confined spaces.</p></p>
