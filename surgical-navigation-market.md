<p><ul><li>Optical Surgical Navigation Systems</li><li>Electromagnetic Surgical Navigation Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The surgical navigation market consists of two primary types: optical and electromagnetic surgical navigation systems. Optical surgical navigation systems use infrared or visible light to track instruments and provide real-time visualization, enhancing precision in procedures. In contrast, electromagnetic surgical navigation systems rely on magnetic fields to detect instruments' positions, allowing for flexibility in various clinical environments. Both systems aim to increase surgical accuracy, improve patient outcomes, and minimize invasiveness during complex surgical procedures across various specialties.</p></p>
