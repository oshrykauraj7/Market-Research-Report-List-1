<p><p>The microfluidic valves market comprises several types that facilitate precise fluid control. Bubble-based valves use gas bubbles to block flow, while capillary burst valves operate when fluid pressure exceeds a certain threshold to release liquid. PDMS-based pneumatic valves leverage air pressure to open or close channels. Electrostatic valves utilize electrical charges for fluid manipulation. Time-delayed valves introduce a delay before fluid flow resumes, whereas electrowetting valves rely on surface tension changes. Other innovative valve types also contribute to this diverse market.</p></p>
