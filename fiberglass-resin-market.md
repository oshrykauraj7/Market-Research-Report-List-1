<p><p>The fiberglass resin market is divided into waterproof and oil-proof categories, each serving distinct applications. Waterproof fiberglass resins are designed to resist moisture, making them ideal for marine, construction, and outdoor products where durability against water exposure is crucial. In contrast, oil-proof fiberglass resins are formulated to withstand petroleum-based substances, making them suitable for automotive, industrial, and oil-related applications where protection against grease and oil is necessary. Both types enhance the longevity and performance of composite materials in their respective uses.</p></p>
